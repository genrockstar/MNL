THE LEGEND OF ETERNAL FIVE

Listen:

Salt & Steel

Long before the arena was built, in the Golden Age of the Sulubasi, the archipelago was a paradise of emerald waters and singing winds. It was protected by five brothers, not bound by blood, but by a covenant stronger than iron: salt in their veins and steel in their spines. They were the Eternal Five: Pablo, the eldest and the strategist; Josh, the mountain of a man; Justin, the gentle soul; Ken, the shadow of the jungle; and Stell, the eagle-eyed marksman.

But paradise is a fragile thing. It was shattered not by magic, but by the groan of metal against reef. Invaders arrived in black, iron-clad ships from the frozen north of Germania. They brought no negotiation, only fire and the desire to conquer. The brothers knew that to save their home, they could not just be guardians; they had to become aspects of war itself.

The Wall and The Soul

The first assault was a tidal wave of steel on the White Beach. Josh did not strike back. He stood at the narrow pass, holding nothing but a massive tower shield forged from the hull of a wrecked ship. He became The Wall. For three days and three nights, he held the pass alone, taking a thousand blows—cannons, swords, and arrows—so his brothers would not have to take one. He was the definition of the Tank: ultimate sacrifice through raw endurance. He fell only when his shield finally shattered, his body a fortress of scars, immovable even in death.

Behind him stood Justin, the Mender. Amidst the fire and blood, Justin refused to carry a weapon. Instead, he possessed a lost, ancient magic, pouring his own life force into his kin. He walked through the arrow-fire untouched, knitting his brothers' flesh back together, healing their broken bodies by unraveling his own spirit. When he finally collapsed, the legend says he died with a smile on his face, his life force completely spent, content in the knowledge that he had bought them time.

The Arrow and The Ghost

As the invasion shifted from brute force to the disciplined, tactical fleets of the French, the brothers changed their tactics. Stell climbed to the highest, jagged cliffs of the coast. He became The Arrow. He did not aim to sink the hulls; he aimed to create terror. Using arrows tipped with captured lightning, he would pin a captain’s sleeve to the mast from a mile away, immobilizing the command and crippling their will before they even made landfall. He fought until his quiver was empty and his fingers bled, raining judgment from the sky until the cliffs themselves crumbled beneath the enemy fire.

Below in the dense, suffocating jungle, Ken became The Ghost. He was the silent hunter, the counter-assassin who ensured the soil itself was protected. While Stell rained terror from above, Ken moved unseen through the trees, guarding the land with silence and blood. Enemy patrols would simply vanish, dragged into the undergrowth, never to be seen again. He became one with the island, fading into the wind as the last of his strength gave out.

The Rise of The Wolf

The defense was coordinated by Pablo, the strategist. At first, he used his intellect, turning the tides and reefs into weapons against the ships. But as he watched his brothers fall one by one—Josh the Wall, Justin the Soul, Stell the Arrow, and Ken the Ghost—something shattered inside him.

The man of intellect died, and a monster was born. He abandoned strategy for pure, primal fury. He became The Wolf. He did not drive back the final Spanish commander with tactics; he drove him back with terrifying, howl-induced rage. He boarded the flagship alone, a whirlwind of claws and mana, tearing the invasion apart from the inside. It was madness, born of grief, that finally cleared the seas.

"Victory came, but Pablo hated the silence that followed. He believed the peace was too costly."

Epilogue: The Arena of Grief

Victory brought no peace to Pablo's heart. Consumed by grief and fury, he feared that time would erase the sacrifice of the Eternal Five. He declared that the archipelago must be divided into three domains: Simula Island (The Beginning), Wakas Island (The End), and Mahalima Island (The Center). To ensure the world never forgot, he built the massive arena on Mahalima, right on his brothers' grave. He set the ancient rules: Lane against Lane, Ancient against Ancient.

The victors of this ritual combat would rule the archipelago for one year, ensuring balance through eternal struggle. This is why the Mahalima Challenge exists. It is not a game. It is a ritual designed by the Wolf to ensure that honor is forged in conflict.
