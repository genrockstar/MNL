Chapter I

Salt & Steel

Long before the arena was built, in the Golden Age of the Sulubasi, there lived five brothers. They were not bound by blood, but by something stronger: salt in their veins and steel in their spines. They were the Eternal Five: Pablo, Josh, Justin, Ken, and Stell.

Their paradise was shattered not by magic, but by iron. Invaders arrived in black, iron-clad ships from Germania, bringing only brute force. The brothers knew that to save their home, they could not just be warriors; they had to become aspects of war itself.

The Wall and The Soul

First, there was Josh. When the brute force of the iron ships crashed against the shore, Josh did not strike back. He stood without a weapon, holding only a massive shield forged from a wrecked hull. He became The Wall. For three days, he held the pass alone, taking a thousand blows so his brothers would not have to take one.

Behind him stood Justin, the Gentle Soul. Amidst the fire and blood, Justin refused to carry a weapon. Instead, he possessed a lost magic, pouring his own life force into his kin. He knitted their flesh back together, healing their broken bodies by unraveling his own spirit. When he fell, the legend says he died with a smile, believing he had saved them all.

The Arrow and The Ghost

As the invasion shifted from brute force to the disciplined fleets of the French, the tactics changed. Stell climbed to the high cliffs. He became The Arrow. Using arrows tipped with lightning, he would pin a captain’s sleeve to the mast from a mile away, immobilizing the command and crippling their will.

Below in the jungle, Ken became The Ghost. He was the silent hunter, the counter-assassin who ensured the soil itself was protected. While Stell rained terror from above, Ken moved unseen through the trees, guarding the land with silence and blood.

The Wolf

The defense was coordinated by Pablo, the strategist. But as he watched his brothers fall, something shattered inside him. The man of intellect died, and a monster was born. He became The Wolf. He did not drive back the commander with tactics; he drove him back with pure, terrifying rage. It was madness, born of grief, that finally cleared the seas.

Epilogue: The Arena

Victory came, but Pablo hated the silence that followed. To ensure the world never forgot their sacrifice, he carved his rage into the islands. He built the arena on Mahalima, right on his brothers' grave. He set the ancient rules: Lane against Lane, Ancient against Ancient.

Chapter II

Child of the Wolf

When the wars were finished and the seas grew quiet, Pablo returned to the Mahalima Sanctuary. It was here, among the stones and salt air, that he found a child left upon his doorstep. No name, no family—only a letter: “I will get him back in the near future.” Pablo took the child in, gave him food, gave him shelter, and raised him as his own.

The Bond

Years passed, and the boy grew under Pablo’s care. The Wolf, who had once carved arenas from grief, now carved a life of quiet guardianship. He gave the boy warmth, guidance, and the strength of discipline.

On the child’s seventh year, Pablo bestowed upon him a gift: a bracelet from the Cave of Moonlight, where the Eternal Five had once sworn their brotherhood beneath silver waters. The boy wore it, unaware of the blood and sacrifice that had forged its meaning.

The Legacy

Pablo never spoke of his past, nor of the ritual battles between Simula and Wakas. Yet he began to teach the boy—first the art of fighting, then the art of thinking. He told him only one truth: “One day, I will not be here. You must protect yourself.”

From seven to twelve, the boy learned blade and mind, endurance and wisdom. He was shaped by Pablo’s grief, but also by his love. And so the tale of the Wolf did not end with rage—it continued with legacy. The child who had been left at the sanctuary’s door, the boy raised in shadow and moonlight... His name was Karle.

Chapter III

School of Simula

When Karle reached twelve years of age, Pablo spoke to him with gravity. "I have another obligation to attend," the Wolf said. "You must go to my brother Josue on Simula Island. You must connect with the people, for strength is not only in arms but in understanding."

Karle frowned. "Why? I don’t need to be in school. I am already ready for anything. I am brawns with brains, you know."

Pablo laughed, his white hair flowing. "You are still a child. There are many things to learn in this life and in this world." Karle sighed, but obeyed.

Four Years in Simula

Time passed. From twelve to sixteen, Karle lived among the Simulayans. He grew close to Josue’s family, playing with his cousins and walking to school. To the people, he was just an ordinary boy—average, quiet, wearing glasses.

He made two friends, Rus and Jai, who became his companions in study and play. Yet Karle kept his secret. When asked about his father, he only said, “He is very busy, so my uncle takes care of me.” He wanted a low-key life, far from the shadow of legends.

The Challenge Revealed

On Karle’s sixteenth year, Pablo’s voice grew solemn. "It is time. You must be ready. The battle of the Mahalima Challenge approaches. Each year, five champions rise from Simula and five from Wakas. They fight upon Mahalima Island, and the victors rule the archipelago for one year."

Karle’s eyes widened. "What is that? The Mahalima Challenge… in Mahalima Island?" And so, the boy who had lived quietly among the Simulayans, the child raised in secrecy, now stood at the threshold of destiny.

Chapter IV

Trial of Youth

At sixteen, Karle’s quiet life in Simula shifted. He sat in class, hearing Pablo’s voice in his mind: "You must prepare." He looked up to realize the words were coming from Ms. Ramos, his teacher.

Ms. Ramos was no ordinary teacher. At fifty years old, she carried the quiet dignity of a woman who had lived through war and healing. "To teach is to heal," she often said. For her, guiding the youth of Simula was the best way to serve the archipelago. To Karle, Rus, and Jai, she was more than a teacher—she was a guardian of their future.

The Five Villages of Simula

  • Aplaya Cove: The Gateway. Bustling harbor of trade, diplomacy, and shipbuilding.
  • Terrasol: The Breadbasket. Fertile plains of agriculture feeding the island.
  • Ironwood Grove: The Makers. Dense rainforest of artisans, hunters, and medicine.
  • The Apex Sanctum: The Keepers. Ascetic scholars and spiritual leaders.
  • Mire’s Edge: The Survivors. Foggy swamps of poisons and resilience.

Each village would send contenders to the coliseum. The five highest scorers would form the team to represent Simula.

The Shadow Above

As the crowd settled in the great coliseum of Simula, a shadow moved across the top. The figure stepped into the light. The Wolf stood in the center. Half-human, half-beast, his face hidden behind a mask.

The people gasped in awe. To them, he was not Pablo. He was only The Wolf. But Karle felt something more. "Why does this figure feel familiar?" he thought. The feeling pressed into him, heavy and unshakable.

Chapter V

The Coliseum Battles

The moon cast long shadows across the ancient ruins. On one side stood Ernel, the mystic mage. Opposite him was Karle, adjusting his glasses. "Your gadgets are no match for the ancient arts," Ernel declared. Karle smirked. "We’ll see about that." The decisive moment came when Karle hurled his Smartphone Strike, blasting Ernel into defeat.

Jai vs. Missy

Missy blurred forward with Deadline Rush, shouting, "Time is money, Jai!" But Jai remained calm. When Missy hurled her Document Blast, Jai’s Lotus Orb reflected it back. THWACK! Missy was struck by her own attack. Jai followed with Guardian Angel, healing himself and knocking her down.

Rus vs. Denice

Denice danced across the arena, using her Heritage Fan to silence Rus. But Rus shook it off. His Jade Carapace reflected her damage. "Nowhere left to run!" Rus roared, unleashing Emerald Overgrowth to root her completely. Denice raised her hand: "I can’t move! I give up!"

The Revelation

Karle sat alone in the preparation chamber. Behind him, a voice broke the silence. "Hey." The Wolf stood there, removing his mask. The face beneath was one Karle knew. "Pablo…" A storm of emotions surged through him—joy, shock, anger. His father was the Wolf.

Semi-Finals: Karle vs. Karlos

Karle faced Karlos, the Gymnastic Dynamo. Karlos launched a Tumble Assault, but Karle didn't panic. He triggered Urban Dash. When Karlos used Gravity Defy to become a golden comet, Karle waited. Fueled by the truth of Pablo’s identity, Karle fought with clarity. Brain beat brawn. Winner: Karle.
