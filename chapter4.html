The Chronicles

TRIAL OF YOUTH

Listen:

At sixteen, Karle’s quiet life in Simula shifted forever. He sat in class, half-listening to the lecture on archipelago economics, half-drifting into memory. In his mind, he heard Pablo’s voice echoing from the night before: "You must prepare. The time has come. At sixteen, you are no longer a child. You must learn not by hearsay, but by trial."

Karle blinked, startled. He thought it was Pablo speaking to him right there in the room. But when he looked up, he realized the words were coming from Ms. Ramos, his teacher. She was staring directly at him, her eyes sharp and knowing. "You have a destiny, Karle," she seemed to imply with her gaze. He realized he had been daydreaming, and Pablo’s lessons had bled into his reality.

Ms. Ramos

Ms. Ramos was no ordinary teacher. At fifty years old, she carried the quiet dignity of a woman who had lived through war and healing. In another country, she had been a renowned medic, saving countless lives on battlefields scarred by fire. But here, in Simula, she chose a different path.

"To teach is to heal," she often said to the class. For her, guiding the youth of Simula was the best way to serve the archipelago. She wanted to give them strength, wisdom, and unity—so that when the time came, they could save their people without bloodshed. Though single and without children of her own, she poured her heart into her students. To Karle, Rus, and Jai, she was more than a teacher—she was a guardian of their future.

The Five Villages of Simula

The time for the selection had come. The archipelago was buzzing with excitement. Each of the five villages would send their best contenders to the coliseum to earn a spot on the team:

  • Aplaya Cove (The Gateway): The bustling harbor of trade, diplomacy, and shipbuilding.
  • Terrasol (The Breadbasket): Fertile plains of agriculture and livestock that fed the island.
  • Ironwood Grove (The Makers): The dense rainforest home of artisans, hunters, and medicine men.
  • The Apex Sanctum (The Keepers): Ascetic scholars and spiritual leaders living on the volcano slopes.
  • Mire’s Edge (The Survivors): The foggy swamps known for poisons, shadows, and resilience.

Each village sent their champions. One-on-one duels would determine who earned points. Victory came when an opponent was struck down or forced to yield. The five highest scorers would form the team to represent Simula in the Mahalima Challenge.

The Shadow Above

As the crowd settled in the great coliseum of Simula, a hush fell over the stands. A shadow moved across the top of the coliseum, climbing toward the high platform reserved for the leaders of Simula. The figure stepped into the light.

The Wolf stood in the center.

He was half-human, half-beast, his face hidden behind a terrifying mask that resembled a snarling wolf. His attire was solemn: a traditional barong tagalog, black pants, and polished black shoes. It was the predator cloaked in tradition.

The people gasped in awe. To them, he was not Pablo. He was only The Wolf, the mysterious guardian of the ritual, the architect of eternal conflict. His real name was forgotten to history.

Karle’s heart pounded against his ribs. He looked up at the figure, his hand tightening around the bracelet of moonlight on his wrist. The crowd saw only a myth. But Karle felt something more. Something in the Wolf’s stance, in the way his masked eyes lingered on the contenders, struck him deeply.

Why does this figure feel familiar? Why does he stir something inside me? Karle did not know the Wolf was Pablo. Not yet. But the feeling pressed into him, heavy and unshakable, a prelude to the truth that was about to break.
